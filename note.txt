1. Shift Management (Clock-In / Clock-Out)
What it does: The login doesn't just open the dashboard; it records the exact time the staff started their shift.
Why it's useful: At the end of the day, you can generate a report showing exactly how many hours each person worked and who was responsible for the cash drawer during any specific hour.
2. Multi-Role Permissions (Admin vs. Counter vs. Godown)
What it does: The login determines what the person can see.
Counter Staff: Can only see Billing and Dashboard.
Admin: Can see everything, including Profit/Loss and Product Costs.
Godown Head: Can only see Stock and Inventory.
Why it's useful: It keeps your sensitive data (like purchase costs and total profit) hidden from regular staff.
3. Session Auto-Lock (Idle Security)
What it does: If the computer is left idle at the billing counter for 5 minutes, it automatically "locks" the screen.
Why it's useful: Prevents customers or unauthorized people from making bills or checking your sales data if the staff walks away for a moment.
4. Attendance & Payroll Integration
What it does: Uses the login data to calculate monthly salary.
Why it's useful: No need for a separate attendance register. You'll know if someone logged in late or left early directly from the sales software.
5. Unclosed Shift Recovery
What it does: If the power goes out or the system crashes, the next time someone logs in, it detects an "Active Unclosed Shift."
Why it's useful: It asks: "You have an unclosed shift from 2:00 PM. Do you want to resume and continue the bills?" This ensures no data is lost between staff changes.
6. Low Stock Alerts on Login
What it does: Immediately after logging in, a popup shows: "Attention: 5 items are below critical stock levels. Notify godown?"
Why it's useful: Ensures the staff knows what they cannot sell before the first customer arrives.
7. Dual-User Authentication (Cashier & Supervisor)
What it does: For things like "Cancelling a Bill," the system requires a Supervisor's login/PIN to approve the action.
Why it's useful: Prevents internal theft where staff might bill a customer and then "cancel" it later to keep the cash.

. WhatsApp "Silent" Marketing
The Idea: Instead of just sending a bill, the software tracks what each customer buys.
Difference: At the end of the week, the software gives you a list: "These 10 customers haven't bought Green Tea in 1 month, but they usually buy it every 20 days."
Action: With one click, it sends them a "We miss you" discount specifically for Green Tea.
